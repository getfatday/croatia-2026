01Mon

Brač Island — Skrip & Postira

Gate 1 Day 4 · Ferry + first hike
Map: Skrip Hotel
Morning
Ferry Split → Supetar

Ferry ~1 hour across to Brač, the third-largest Adriatic island.

Midday
Skrip & the Olive Oil Museum

Village visit

Skrip is the oldest village on Brač — Illyrian, Roman, then Venetian layers stacked on top of each other. Small Olive Oil Museum walks through how the island's stone-walled groves have worked for centuries.

~1:00pm
Lunch at a local restaurant

Included Konoba-style meal with the group.

Afternoon
Hike to Postira

Moderate hike

3.7 mi · 3 hrs · ±1,650 ft down toward the north coast. Postira is a fishing village with a small harbor.

Walking poles are recommended for the tour as a whole. Rocky paths, lots of limestone.
Evening
Supetar — Aminess Vival Velaris

Check into the seaside hotel in Supetar for two nights.

02Tue

Vidova Gora & Zlatni Rat

Highest peak in the Adriatic islands
Map: Vidova Gora Zlatni Rat
Morning
Hike up Vidova Gora

Moderate hike

4.3 mi · 3 hrs · ±755 ft from Podgazul Village up to Vidova Gora — 778 m (2,552 ft), the highest point on any Adriatic island. The final ridge looks straight down onto Zlatni Rat and across to Hvar.

Midday
Picnic at the summit

Included Packed lunch with the view.

Afternoon
Descend to Zlatni Rat Beach

Beach

Zlatni Rat — "Golden Horn" — is the famous pebble spit that shifts shape with the wind and current. Water is clear turquoise. Bring swim shoes; it's smooth stones, not sand.

Late afternoon
Wine tasting at a local household

Family-hosted Gate 1 arranges a visit to a Brač household — local wines, stories, snacks.

03Wed

Korčula — Marco Polo's island

Catamaran + vineyard hike
Map: Korčula Old Town Pupnat
Morning
Catamaran Brač → Korčula

Catamaran Fast boat down the Dalmatian coast.

Midday
Hike from Pupnat through the vineyards

Challenging hike

6.9 mi · 4 hrs · ±1,260 ft. The path climbs out of Pupnat through dry-stone vineyards and drops down toward Žrnovo with archipelago views the whole way.

Afternoon
Taste local products in Žrnovo

Included Žrnovo is the village above Korčula town. Olive oil, cheese, local wines.

Late afternoon
Descend into Korčula Old Town

Historic

The walled old town sits on a small peninsula — a fishbone street grid designed to catch the breeze and block the wind. Local tradition says Marco Polo was born here in 1254.

Evening
Aminess Korčula Heritage Hotel

Two nights on Korčula.

04Thu

Mljet National Park

Two saltwater lakes & a 12th-century monastery
Map: Mljet NP
Morning
Catamaran to Mljet

Catamaran Day trip from Korčula.

All day
Hike the national park

Challenging hike

5.6 mi · 4 hrs through pine forest to the two saltwater lakes — Veliko Jezero and Malo Jezero. In the middle of Veliko, Sveta Marija (St. Mary's Island) holds a 12th-century Benedictine monastery.

Swim break in the lakes if the group has time — the water is warmer than the open sea.
Evening
Dinner at a traditional restaurant

Included Gate 1 dinner, then catamaran back to Korčula for the night.

Dubrovnik Old Town

Walking tour + free afternoon
Map: Dubrovnik Old Town GoT: Dubrovnik Old Town Hotel
Morning
Transfer to Dubrovnik

Transfer From Korčula down to Dubrovnik. Check into Valamar Lacroma Dubrovnik (Iva Dulčića 34, Babin Kuk).

Midday
Walking tour of the Old Town

UNESCO

Rector's Palace, Sponza Palace, Orlando Tower on the Stradun, Dominican Monastery, and the Franciscan Monastery with its 14th-century pharmacy — one of the oldest still operating in the world.

Afternoon
Free afternoon — open question

City Walls, Mt. Srđ cable car, or Lokrum Island. Cast your vote ↓

If the walls win — they take ~2 hours and there's no shade. Bring water.
Evening
Group dinner

Included Gate 1 dinner in Dubrovnik.

06Sat

Kotor day trip

Self-organized · Gate 1 Day 9 is a leisure day
Map: Kotor Directions
Before 8:00am
Leave Dubrovnik for Montenegro

Border crossing

~2 to 2.5 hours each way — by rental car or organized tour. Vote on the mode ↓

Passports. US citizens don't need a visa. Border wait can add 30–60 min each way — early June should be lighter than peak summer, but don't count on zero.
Midday
Walk Kotor Old Town

UNESCO

Tight medieval stone alleys tucked against the mountainside. Several small churches and the Maritime Museum if you're into it.

Afternoon
Climb the fortress walls

Steep climb

San Giovanni (St. John's) Fortress — the zigzag walls up the mountain. Full climb is 1,350 steps with views over the Bay of Kotor. Go as far as you like and turn back.

Late afternoon
Lunch by the water & return

On your own

Back to Dubrovnik by evening. Gate 1 breakfast is included, but lunch and dinner are on your own today.
